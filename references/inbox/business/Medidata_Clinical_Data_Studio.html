Medidata Clinical Data Studio (CDS) connects data from Medidata and external sources into a single review and monitoring environment to maintain quality and keep trials on track.

FAQ

Can Clinical Data Studio integrate data from non-Medidata sources?

Yes. Clinical Data Studio is designed to ingest, standardize, and integrate data from multiple sources, including both Medidata and non-Medidata systems. It enables self-service data import and validation, allowing data managers and monitors to review all clinical trial data in a single, unified location, regardless of source.

How does Clinical Data Studio utilize AI to improve data quality?

The solution leverages AI and automation to streamline data surveillance and quality monitoring workflows. Specific AI capabilities include assisting with data reconciliation and offering a generative AI-powered audit trail review, which allows users to interrogate audit trail data using natural language without needing assistance from clinical programmers.

What specific efficiency metrics or time savings can study teams expect?

By utilizing Clinical Data Studio, organizations have reported significant reductions in manual effort, including up to a 90% reduction in time for generating listings and an 80% reduction in data review cycle times, per cycle. 

Does Clinical Data Studio support risk-based quality management (RBQM)?

Yes. Clinical Data Studio centralizes risk-based strategies by allowing clinical operations teams to configure and monitor key risk indicators (KRIs) and quality tolerance limits (QTLs) directly within the platform. It uses AI and automation to detect anomalies, identify site performance risks, and uncover patient safety issues earlier in the process.  

Does Medidata offer training for its clients and partners?

Yes. Medidata offers a variety of training options for our clients and partners, including both self-paced and instructor-led courses. To learn more about available courses and to access our resources, please visit the Medidata Global Education and Training section.
